# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Last Updated:** December 2025
**Version:** 1.0

## Overview

This Acceptable Use Policy defines appropriate and inappropriate use of company systems, networks, and data. All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to company resources must comply with this policy.

**Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and legal action.**

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All company employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Third-party vendors with system access
- Any individual granted access to company systems or student data

This policy covers:
- Company-provided computers, devices, and equipment
- Company networks (office, VPN, cloud services)
- Company email and communication systems
- Student data and university partner information
- All systems used to access student education records

## Acceptable Uses

### You MAY:

**Data Access:**
- ✅ Access student data necessary for your specific job duties
- ✅ Access data within your approved access tier only
- ✅ Use approved systems and software for work tasks
- ✅ Share student information with authorized colleagues on a strict need-to-know basis
- ✅ Access systems from approved locations and devices

**Communications:**
- ✅ Use company email for business purposes
- ✅ Use approved communication channels (Slack, email, etc.)
- ✅ Encrypt sensitive data before transmission
- ✅ Use secure file sharing methods approved by IT

**Devices:**
- ✅ Use company-provided devices for work
- ✅ Use personal devices ONLY if approved and properly secured
- ✅ Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- ✅ Keep software and security patches up to date

**Security Practices:**
- ✅ Use strong, unique passwords
- ✅ Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all accounts
- ✅ Lock your workstation when away from desk
- ✅ Report security incidents immediately
- ✅ Verify identity before sharing sensitive information

## Prohibited Uses

### You MAY NOT:

**Unauthorized Access:**
- ❌ Access student data out of curiosity or personal interest
- ❌ Access data beyond your approved access tier
- ❌ Attempt to access systems or data you're not authorized to view
- ❌ Share login credentials with anyone (including colleagues)
- ❌ Use another employee's account or credentials
- ❌ Attempt to bypass security controls or authentication

**Data Handling Violations:**
- ❌ Download student data to personal devices without authorization
- ❌ Store student data on unapproved cloud services (Dropbox, personal Google Drive, etc.)
- ❌ Email student PII without encryption
- ❌ Take screenshots of student data for personal use
- ❌ Print student records without legitimate need
- ❌ Leave printed student records unattended
- ❌ Discuss student information in public areas or on social media
- ❌ Share student data with family, friends, or unauthorized individuals

**Device Misuse:**
- ❌ Install unauthorized software on company devices
- ❌ Disable antivirus or security software
- ❌ Connect to unsecured public Wi-Fi without VPN
- ❌ Use personal USB drives on company computers (without approval)
- ❌ Store student data on removable media (unless encrypted)

**Communication Violations:**
- ❌ Send confidential data via unencrypted email
- ❌ Use personal email for work involving student data
- ❌ Forward company or student information to personal accounts
- ❌ Click on suspicious links or download unknown attachments
- ❌ Respond to phishing attempts

**Illegal or Harmful Activities:**
- ❌ Access, download, or distribute illegal content
- ❌ Harass, threaten, or abuse others via company systems
- ❌ Intentionally introduce malware or viruses
- ❌ Conduct unauthorized security testing or penetration testing
- ❌ Use systems for personal business or financial gain
- ❌ Violate copyright or intellectual property laws

## Internet and Email Usage

### Permitted Use
- **Primary Purpose:** Business use
- **Limited Personal Use:** Minimal personal use permitted during breaks (no student data involved)
- **Monitoring:** All internet and email usage may be monitored

### Prohibited Activities
- Accessing inappropriate, offensive, or illegal websites
- Downloading unauthorized software or files
- Excessive personal use that interferes with work
- Streaming content excessively (bandwidth concerns)
- Participating in illegal file sharing

### Email Security
- **Phishing Awareness:** Do not click links or attachments from unknown senders
- **Verification:** Verify unusual requests (especially financial) via another channel
- **Sensitive Data:** Never email unencrypted student PII
- **Personal Email:** Do not forward work emails to personal accounts

## Remote Work Requirements

If working remotely, you must:
- ✅ Use company-approved VPN for all remote access
- ✅ Ensure secure home network (WPA2/WPA3 encryption)
- ✅ Never use public Wi-Fi without VPN
- ✅ Work in private areas where others cannot view screen
- ✅ Use privacy screen filters on laptops
- ✅ Lock devices when not in use
- ✅ Secure physical workspace (no documents left visible)

## FERPA-Specific Requirements

When handling student education records:

**Before Access:**
- Verify you have completed FERPA training
- Confirm you have legitimate educational interest
- Ensure access is within your job duties

**During Access:**
- Access minimum necessary data only
- Do not access student records of family members, friends, or yourself
- Do not share screen during video calls unless necessary and approved
- Use privacy screens in public/shared spaces

**After Access:**
- Log out completely when finished
- Do not retain copies of student data unnecessarily
- Follow data retention and deletion policies

**Disclosure:**
- Only disclose student PII when FERPA permits
- Verify recipient is authorized before sharing
- Document any disclosures as required by university policy
- Never post student information on social media

## Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

Personal devices may ONLY be used for work if:
- ✅ Approved in writing by IT/Security team
- ✅ Device meets security requirements (encryption, MFA, etc.)
- ✅ Mobile Device Management (MDM) software installed
- ✅ Agreement signed acknowledging company monitoring rights
- ✅ Device used for approved purposes only

**Even with approval:**
- ❌ Do NOT store student PII on personal devices
- ❌ Do NOT use personal cloud storage for student data
- ✅ Accept that work data may be remotely wiped if device is lost

## Mobile Device Security

If using mobile devices (company or personal) for work:
- Enable device encryption
- Set strong passcode/biometric lock
- Enable automatic lock after 5 minutes idle
- Keep OS and apps updated
- Only install apps from official stores
- Report lost/stolen devices immediately
- Allow remote wipe capability

## Monitoring and Privacy

**Employee Awareness:**
- Company systems and networks are monitored
- Email, internet usage, and system access are logged
- Monitoring is for security, compliance, and policy enforcement
- No expectation of privacy on company systems
- Monitoring may be reviewed during investigations

**What We Monitor:**
- Login attempts and access patterns
- Email sent/received through company accounts
- Internet browsing history
- File downloads and transfers
- Database queries and data access
- System resource usage

## Social Media and Public Communications

**Personal Social Media:**
- Do not post student information
- Do not post confidential company information
- Do not post screenshots of work systems
- Do not claim to speak for the company

**Work-Related Social Media:**
- Only authorized personnel may post on behalf of company
- Never share student data or university partner information
- Follow company social media guidelines

## Reporting Violations

**If you witness a violation of this policy:**
1. Report immediately to your manager or security team
2. Email: security@[company].com
3. Hotline: [phone number]

**If you accidentally violate this policy:**
1. Report immediately (self-reporting may reduce consequences)
2. Stop the violating activity
3. Cooperate fully with investigation

**Protected Reporting:**
- No retaliation for good-faith reporting
- Anonymous reporting options available
- Reports investigated promptly and confidentially

## Consequences of Violations

Violations are categorized by severity:

| Severity | Examples | Potential Consequences |
|----------|----------|----------------------|
| **Minor** | Weak password, unlocked workstation | Warning, additional training |
| **Moderate** | Unencrypted email with PII, unapproved software | Written warning, temporary access restriction |
| **Major** | Unauthorized data access, sharing credentials | Suspension, access revocation, possible termination |
| **Critical** | Data theft, intentional breach, FERPA violation | Immediate termination, legal action, criminal prosecution |

**All violations are documented and may be reported to:**
- University partners (for FERPA violations)
- Law enforcement (for criminal activity)
- U.S. Department of Education (for FERPA violations)

## Exceptions and Approvals

**Requesting an exception:**
- Submit written request to security team
- Provide business justification
- Await written approval before proceeding
- Approval may include conditions and monitoring

**Temporary exceptions:**
- May be granted for special projects
- Time-limited and documented
- Subject to additional monitoring

## Acknowledgment

By accessing company systems, you acknowledge:
- I have read and understand this Acceptable Use Policy
- I agree to comply with all requirements
- I understand the consequences of violations
- I will report violations and security incidents
- I understand my access may be monitored
- I have completed required security training
