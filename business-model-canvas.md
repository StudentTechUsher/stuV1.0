# Business Model Canvas

## Customer Segments

### **1. State & Public Universities**
Large institutions with significant enrollment and existing SIS/degree planning infrastructure. Heavy advisor workload, high focus on graduation and retention metrics. Budget usually comes from academic affairs, student success divisions, or state/federal grants.

### **2. Community & Technical Colleges**
Mid-sized institutions with constrained budgets but acute advisor bandwidth issues. Strong ROI focus on retention and course demand forecasting. Faster decision cycles and willingness to pilot new tools.

### **3. For-Profit & Online Universities**
High revenue sensitivity with strong incentives around completion rates and student retention. Less legacy system lock-in. Rapid decision-making and quick to scale pilots across distributed student populations.

### **4. Online/Distributed Learning Programs**
Within any institution type—international students, online cohorts, competency-based programs. High self-service dependency, complex scheduling constraints, lower advisor interaction frequency.

---

## Priority Recommendation

**Start with Community & Technical Colleges.** They have the highest pain (budget constraints + advisor overload), shortest sales cycles, and are most willing to be reference customers. Success here creates a low-cost proof point to scale to public universities and for-profit institutions.

# Jobs-To-Be-Done

## **Community & Technical Colleges**
- **Functional Job:** Automate repetitive scheduling and degree-audit tasks so advisors can focus on at-risk students instead of manual planning.
- **Social/Emotional:** Be seen as innovators adopting scalable solutions despite budget constraints.
- **Key Opportunity:** Current systems (Banner, Degree Works) require manual workarounds; Stu eliminates the gap.

## **For-Profit & Online Universities**
- **Functional Job:** Improve completion rates and retention through proactive student guidance and clear graduation pathways.
- **Social/Emotional:** Demonstrate enrollment efficiency and student success metrics to stakeholders and boards.
- **Key Opportunity:** Distributed students lack personalized advising; Stu scales that with automation.

## **State & Public Universities**
- **Functional Job:** Integrate course demand forecasting into planning to optimize enrollment and course capacity.
- **Social/Emotional:** Position as forward-thinking institution managing growth and retention effectively.
- **Key Opportunity:** Siloed planning tools (Degree Works, Banner, CourseDog) don't talk; Stu creates visibility.

## **Online/Distributed Programs**
- **Functional Job:** Enable self-service schedule building with institutional guardrails so students progress without bottlenecking advisors.
- **Social/Emotional:** Feel confident in their graduation timeline despite minimal advisor contact.
- **Key Opportunity:** Generic scheduling tools don't account for degree requirements; Stu personalizes both.

# Customer Value Proposition

## **Community & Technical Colleges**
- **Core Solution:** AI-powered degree planning and schedule automation that frees advisors from low-value scheduling tasks to focus on retention and at-risk students.
- **Key Differentiator:** Purpose-built for budget-constrained institutions; integrates with existing Banner/Degree Works stacks without expensive rip-and-replace.
- **Main Benefit:** 40%+ reduction in advisor time per student on scheduling; immediate retention ROI through proactive intervention.

## **For-Profit & Online Universities**
- **Core Solution:** Personalized graduation roadmaps and semester schedules that guide distributed students with minimal advisor intervention.
- **Key Differentiator:** Scales high-touch advising to thousands of remote students without proportional headcount increases.
- **Main Benefit:** Measurable improvement in completion rates and lower student churn through clear, adaptive guidance.

## **State & Public Universities**
- **Core Solution:** Unified visibility across degree requirements, course demand, and student plans to forecast enrollment and optimize course offerings.
- **Key Differentiator:** Only solution that bridges siloed planning tools; uses AI to generate demand forecasts from real student plans, not historical guesses.
- **Main Benefit:** Data-driven enrollment planning and course scheduling that reduces empty seats and improves institutional efficiency.

## **Online/Distributed Programs**
- **Core Solution:** Self-service graduation planner that ensures compliance with degree requirements while respecting student constraints.
- **Key Differentiator:** Combines institutional guardrails (policies, prerequisites) with student agency—not a rigid template.
- **Main Benefit:** Students feel empowered and confident; advisors only intervene on exceptions, not every schedule request.

# Channels

## **Acquisition**
- **Direct Outbound to Decision-Makers (LinkedIn, Email, Phone):** Registrars, provosts, and student success directors at community colleges and public universities respond best to personalized, problem-focused outreach tied to retention metrics and advisor ROI.
- **Professional Conferences & Associations (NACADA, AACRAO):** Community colleges and public universities actively attend these conferences; exhibits and speaking slots position Stu as an insider solution, not external hype.

## **Delivery**
- **SaaS Cloud Platform (Hosted):** All segments prefer Stu hosted externally to avoid integration complexity and IT overhead; institutions provide data via API or secure bulk export.
- **Pilot-First Model:** Start with single advising center or student cohort (online students, freshmen) to reduce perceived risk and prove ROI before institution-wide rollout.

## **Support**
- **Dedicated Customer Success Manager (Hybrid Live + Self-Service):** Community colleges and for-profits value personal onboarding and quarterly check-ins; larger public universities can lean on self-service documentation and Slack support.

# Revenue Streams

## **Primary Revenue Model**
Annual SaaS subscription with tiered feature sets and usage-based setup/integration fees.

## **Pricing Approach**
Per-enrolled-student or per-FTE model with institutional tiers; allows budget-constrained community colleges to start small (single cohort) while supporting large public universities at scale.

## **Revenue per Segment**

**Community & Technical Colleges:** $8–15/student/year for core graduation planning + scheduling; integration fee ($5K–15K) to connect to Banner/Degree Works.

**For-Profit & Online Universities:** $12–20/student/year; higher tier includes advanced retention analytics and real-time churn alerts; integration fees ($15K–30K) offset by faster ROI.

**State & Public Universities:** $6–12/student/year (volume discount) for full suite including course demand forecasting; enterprise support ($50K–100K) and custom integrations billed separately.

**Online/Distributed Programs:** Freemium student-facing tool (ad-supported or upsell to institution) + institutional premium for advisor dashboards and reporting ($3–8/student/year).

# Cost Structure

## **Top Cost Categories**
1. **Engineering/Product Salaries:** $250K–300K/year (4–6 engineers + PM, largest fixed cost)
2. **Customer Success & Sales:** $120K–150K/year (founder + 1 sales rep + 1 CSM)
3. **Cloud Infrastructure (AWS/Supabase):** $5K–15K/month (scales with data volume and API calls)
4. **Customer Acquisition (Sales/Marketing):** $50K–80K/year (conferences, LinkedIn ads, travel)
5. **Operations (Legal, Finance, Admin):** $40K–60K/year

## **Fixed vs. Variable Split**
~75% fixed (salaries, office, baseline infrastructure); ~25% variable (cloud costs scale with customer usage; CAC per pilot = ~$10K–20K).

## **Cost Strategy**
Value-driven (invest in AI/ML quality and CSM depth to justify premium pricing and reduce churn).

## **Break-Even Estimate**
~8–12 mid-size community college customers at $60K–100K ACV (including integration fees) + ~50K–100K students generating recurring subscription revenue. Monthly burn rate: ~$40K–50K; break-even in 18–24 months with aggressive pilot-to-expansion conversion.

# Business Model Review & Validation Plan

## **Coherence Assessment**
Your model is internally consistent and well-aligned. Jobs-to-be-done (advisor time savings, completion rate improvement, forecasting visibility) directly map to CVP (automation, retention analytics, unified dashboards), and your pilot-first channels + high-touch CSM support the promise of fast implementation. One gap: the freemium Online/Distributed Programs strategy feels adjacent to your core B2B motion; consider whether this truly serves institutional customers or dilutes focus on 8–12 paying community college pilots.

## **Biggest Risk**
**Data integration complexity & speed-to-value.** You promise 2-week pilots, but institutional data is messy—transcript formats vary, degree requirements are institution-specific, and Banner/Degree Works APIs are inconsistent. One delayed pilot or failed integration significantly impacts your 90-day ROI proof point and founder sales cycle.

## **Key Strength**
**Founder domain expertise + AI/ML moat.** You have authentic advisor/registrar knowledge that competitors lack, paired with a defensible AI scheduling engine—this justifies your premium pricing and co-creation relationships.

## **Financial Viability**
Unit economics are attractive: at $60K–100K ACV with $5K–15K/month cloud costs per customer, you break even at ~10 institutions. However, your 18–24 month break-even depends on: (1) 40% expansion conversion from pilot to full institution, and (2) maintaining <$40K/month burn rate—both challenging with high-touch CSM costs.

## **Top 3 Validation Priorities**

1. **Validate 2-week integration & 40% time savings claim:** Run a real 90-day pilot with 1–2 champion advisors at a friendly community college; measure actual advisor hours saved and data integration time.

2. **Test CAC recovery in pilots:** Confirm that integration fees ($5K–15K) + early-year subscription revenue covers the $10K–20K CAC per pilot within 12–18 months.

3. **Validate expansion economics:** Identify if pilot advisors actually evangelize to provost/registrar for full-institution rollout, or if you must re-sell separately (impacts sales cycle and expansion revenue).
